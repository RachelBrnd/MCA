Similarity

The below visuals help us to calculate the similarity between 10 tracks in three different genres. Tracks 0-3 were classical, 4-6 were rock and 7-9 were electronic. Each track was downloaded from the Free Music Archive

To view the full fileset for this task, and the python script used to produce these graphics please visit "Project Files" in My GitHub repository.

Notes A and C Occurance Chart

This visual shows the relative occurance of A and C notes in the tracks.
From this one notices that the classical tracks are very similar
in their occurances of these notes. The rock tracks are also generally similar,
although one track has a much higher occurance of A notes than the other two.
Two of the electronic tracks are closely related, although one has a
similar occurance of A notes but a much higher occurance of C notes than the other two.

Notes A and C Distance

This graph shows the distance between tracks, in terms of their
relative occurrance of A and C notes, the warmer the square is - the further
apart the tracks. We can observe groupings between the classical
tracks (0-3) by the dark section in the upper left corner. Elsewhere,
tracks 3 (classical) and 9 (electronic) are the most dissimilar,
with a distance of nearly 1.o between them.



All Notes Distance

This visual shows the distance between tracks when looking at the occurance of all notes.
It's patterns are similar to those visible in the above graph, which only analyses
A and C notes. This suggests that analysis of just two notes can be an accurate indicator
of the closeness of different pieces of music.
